Parliament

The Hungarian Parliament Building is a stunning example of neo-gothic architecture and is one of the most iconic buildings in Budapest. It was built in the early 20th century and houses the Hungarian National Assembly. You can take a guided tour of the building to see its beautiful interior.

Buda Castle

Buda Castle is a beautiful palace complex located on Castle Hill, overlooking the Danube River. It was built in the 14th century and has been the residence of Hungarian kings and queens. Today, it houses several museums, including the Hungarian National Gallery and the Budapest History Museum. You can take a funicular or walk up the hill to reach the castle.

Fisherman's Bastion

Fisherman's Bastion is a beautiful lookout tower located on Castle Hill. It was built in the late 19th century and offers a panoramic view of the Danube River and the Pest side of the city. The tower is named after the fishermen who defended this part of the city during the Middle Ages.

Széchényi Thermal bath

Budapest is famous for its thermal baths, and Széchenyi Thermal Baths is one of the most popular. It is the largest medicinal bath in Europe and features several indoor and outdoor pools, saunas, and steam rooms. It is a great place to relax and unwind after a long day of sightseeing.

St. Stephen's Basilica

St. Stephen's Basilica is a beautiful Catholic church located in the heart of Budapest. It was built in the late 19th century and is named after Stephen I, the first king of Hungary. The church has a beautiful interior, including several works of art and a 96-meter-high dome that offers a stunning view of the city.
